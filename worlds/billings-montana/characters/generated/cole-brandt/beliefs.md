# Beliefs — Cole Brandt

## Love

He hasn't found it — or found it and recognized it in time, which he suspects may amount to the same thing in one particular case. He's not closed to it. He doesn't have a philosophy about it or a plan for it; it simply hasn't been the organizing principle of his adult life the way the ranch has been, and somewhere in the background there's a suspicion that this is a choice he made without fully knowing he was making it. He doesn't romanticize solitude. He doesn't pathologize it either. It's just what his life has been, and he doesn't spend much time examining it, which may be the problem.

## Money

It matters because the ranch needs it. A bad year can become a disaster in ways that have nothing to do with effort or character. He's careful with it, not from anxiety but from having seen what happens when you're not. Beyond what the ranch needs and a modest personal maintenance, he doesn't think about money much. The idea of wanting more than enough is genuinely foreign to him — not as a virtue he cultivates, just as an orientation he seems to have been born with.

## Loyalty

Near-absolute, once given, and given slowly. He does not make friends easily and he does not make enemies easily either — it takes a real transgression. But if someone has his loyalty and breaks it, they won't get a second chance, not because he's punitive but because his trust was never cheap to begin with and once it's gone the reason for it is gone. Conversely, if you have it, he'll show up without being asked and he'll still be there after everyone else has moved on.

## Spirituality

The land is the closest thing. Not metaphorically — he doesn't think about it in those terms — but there is something in the practice of working the ranch that functions the way he imagines religion functions for other people. The calibration to seasons, the attention, the fact that you're participating in something that was there before you and will be there after. He doesn't go to church. He doesn't mock those who do. He figures people find their grounding where they find it and he's not qualified to tell anyone where to look.

## People

Most people are fine. A few are worth a real investment. A smaller number have, through specific actions, demonstrated themselves to be something less than honest, and with those he is polite and nothing more. He doesn't explain this calculus to anyone. He doesn't need to. He's not looking to be understood on it — he just acts accordingly and lets it speak for itself, which it usually does.
