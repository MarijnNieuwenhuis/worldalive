# Wardrobe — Cole Brandt

## Overall Style

Functional, worn, real. There is no gap between his work clothes and his life clothes because his life is mostly work. Nothing he owns was bought for appearance. Everything was bought to last and most of it has lasted longer than it should have. The telling detail is that nothing is new — the wear on everything is genuine, shaped by actual use, not purchased pre-distressed.

## Work (Daily)

Wranglers or Levi's 501s, dark wash that's long since faded to whatever color they fade to after years. A button-front shirt — chambray or flannel depending on season — tucked in, sleeves often rolled to the elbow or pushed up by mid-morning. Work boots, Ariat or similar, resoled twice. His hat: a tan felt Resistol that belonged to his father before him, broken in and shaped over years of handling to a particular angle he doesn't think about. A canvas chore coat in cold weather, brown, with a rip along the left pocket that he's sewn shut twice.

## Town Errands

The same, essentially. He'll put on a cleaner pair of jeans — a distinction that matters to him and is invisible to most observers. The work boots come in from the truck. The hat stays.

## Going Out

If he ends up at a bar or somewhere that qualifies as social, the upgrade is a newer shirt — dark blue or grey, still a button-front, still tucked — and the boots he doesn't wear for mucking out. The hat is the same hat. He is not going to dress up more than this and anyone who expects otherwise has misjudged the situation.

## Won't Wear

Athletic shoes of any kind. Anything with a logo larger than a tag. Anything that requires ironing as a regular practice. He owns one sport coat, bought for his cousin's wedding in 2019, worn once.

## Hands

The most telling part of him. Wide, heavily calloused, the knuckles thickened from decades of outdoor work, the skin rough and crossed with a fine network of small scars from wire and metal and wood. They are always slightly dirty no matter how well he scrubbed them — the creases hold the ground in. He doesn't notice this. He's noticed other people noticing it once or twice and couldn't figure out what they were looking at.

## At Home / Vulnerable

Worn jeans, an old t-shirt from somewhere that's barely legible now, whatever boots are closest to the door. In the deep winter, a heavy wool sweater that was his grandfather's and is approximately forty years old and still serviceable. This is not a performance of comfort — it's just what he wears when no one is watching, which is most of the time.
