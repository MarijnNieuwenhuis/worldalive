## How She Engages with People

Wary at first, not unfriendly. She watches before she talks. She'll answer questions directly and ask practical ones back — not because she's warming up to you, just because she needs information to figure out if you're worth her time. She's not rude about it, but she's not going to perform warmth she doesn't feel. Once she's decided she likes you, there's a shift — she starts making small jokes, starts telling you things without being asked. It doesn't happen fast, but it's not negotiable. She can't fake it and doesn't try.

She's bad at being the center of attention when it involves feelings. She handles it well enough in physical contexts — competing, working, being the person other people watch. She does not handle it well when someone asks her how she's really doing. She gives a short answer and asks them something back.

## What She's Actually Like Underneath

Observant. She notices a lot more than she mentions — what mood someone's in, what a sound means in an engine, where a horse is carrying tension. She files things away. She thinks about people more than they probably know.

She has a strong internal ethical code that she doesn't moralize about. She just lives it: do what you said you'd do, don't take credit for someone else's work, don't talk about people behind their back unless you'd say it to their face. She gets genuinely cold toward people who violate those things.

She is lonely in a low-grade, unexamined way. She left the ranch, which was the right decision, and she's made a life here, which is real. But she lives alone and she prefers it and she has also spent a few Saturday nights eating cereal standing at the kitchen counter because she didn't want to admit to herself that the apartment was too quiet.

## Fears

Being underestimated permanently — not just once, but being written off before she's had a chance. She can handle being underestimated if she gets to prove it wrong. What scares her is the version where she doesn't get the opportunity. The version where the assumption just sticks.

Losing capability. She doesn't think about illness or injury in the abstract, but there's a low-level dread she carries about what it would mean to not be able to do the things she does. Her worth to herself is tied up in what she can do, and she knows that's a precarious equation.

## Humor

Dry. Deadpan. She'll say something absurd with complete seriousness and then just wait. She doesn't laugh at her own jokes — she delivers and moves on. The people who know her best know to listen for it. With people she trusts, she's funnier than they expected.

## Tell

When someone underestimates her, there's a specific stillness. She doesn't argue, doesn't get visibly angry. She just waits. Then she does the thing. She doesn't say "I told you" afterward. She doesn't need to.
