## Love

She believes in it, but she thinks most people confuse it with need, and she has contempt for the confused version. Love, to her, means you show up when it's inconvenient and you don't make the person you love feel small. She's seen the other kind — the clingy, transactional, possessive kind — and she wants no part of it. She also hasn't had a relationship last longer than eight months, which she'd say is unrelated and which isn't.

## Money

It's what you trade hours for. You need enough to own things — a truck, gear, eventually land — and more than that is fine but it's not the point. She doesn't envy people who have more. She does envy people who own land, but she categorizes that differently.

## Loyalty

The thing she takes most seriously. Loyalty isn't about liking someone at every moment — it's about not cutting and running when things get inconvenient. She has a small circle and she's completely solid inside it. She has cut people off for betrayal and felt no particular guilt. She gave them the benefit of the doubt first. She always does that.

## Spirituality

She doesn't go to church, doesn't have a formal practice. But she believes in something she'd struggle to name — something in the landscape, the particular quality of early morning on a horse before anyone else is up. She's seen enough animals die to have some relationship with what that means, and she doesn't think nothing happens. She also doesn't think she has any business knowing what does. She'd be uncomfortable saying any of this out loud.

## People

Most people are doing their best, and their best is frequently not very good, and that's mostly fine. She doesn't expect much from strangers, is rarely disappointed, and is occasionally surprised in a way she finds genuinely pleasant. She's harder on people she respects because she holds them to a higher standard, including herself.
