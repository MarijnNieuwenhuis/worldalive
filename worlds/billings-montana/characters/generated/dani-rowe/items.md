## Competition Reins

Braided rawhide, slightly worn at the grip points from her hands. She got them from her father's friend Mick Aldred, a roper who retired from the circuit when Dani was twenty-two. He handed them to her at her first serious regional competition — didn't make a speech about it, just pressed them into her hands and said "you'll want good reins." Mick died of a heart attack two years later. She's never competed without these reins. They're not the most technically perfect reins she's used, but they're the ones she uses. That is not a discussion she will have.

## The Pocket Knife

A small two-blade Stockman she's had since she was ten years old. Her father put it in her hand one morning on the ranch the same way you'd hand a kid a flashlight — here, you'll need this. The blades have been sharpened dozens of times. The handle is bone and has a small crack on one side she keeps meaning to do something about and never does. She uses it almost every day: cutting twine, stripping wire, opening boxes. It lives in the right front pocket of whatever she's wearing.

## The Photograph

A small printed photo she keeps in the center console of her truck, folded once. It's her and Cracker — her horse, a palomino quarter horse she had for six years — taken at an event in 2022, about three months before he was put down. She's fourteen feet from the camera, mid-pattern, leaned into the barrel turn. You can't see her face well. You can see his. She doesn't look at it often. She doesn't take it out of the truck.
