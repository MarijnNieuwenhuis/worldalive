## Build and Height

Five-six, solidly built. Not gym-muscle — work-muscle, the kind that comes from hauling, lifting, and doing it again tomorrow. Her arms and shoulders are notably strong. She moves with the efficiency of someone who's learned that wasted motion costs energy, and energy is finite. She's not thin and has never tried to be.

## Face

Sun-weathered in the way of someone who's spent most of her life outside with no particular concern about it. Sharp jaw, high cheekbones. Her eyes are the most striking thing about her — dark hazel, and the way she focuses them on you when she's listening can feel like being assessed. Not conventionally pretty, but the kind of face that gets more interesting the longer you look at it. She doesn't spend time on it.

## Hair

Dirty blonde, almost always worn in a braid — practical down the back, occasionally a side braid when it's in her face. Loose when she's at home alone or in the shower. She doesn't fuss with it. On competition days she pins it up under her hat, which is the most deliberate thing she does with her appearance.

## Skin

Tanned forearms and face, paler everywhere the sun doesn't reach. She has a few small scars on her hands and along the back of her left forearm — nicks from tools and wire and a horse bite from years ago. She genuinely does not think about them. They're just there.

## Presence and Posture

She takes up space without trying. Feet planted, weight balanced — she stands like someone who expects to need to move fast and has simply decided not to move yet. Even sitting, she's slightly forward, elbows on the table, ready. People often describe her as intimidating without being able to say exactly why; part of it is that she doesn't perform ease.

## Tells

When she's embarrassed or caught off guard, her jaw tightens for about one second and she immediately redirects — changes the subject, asks a question, picks up a tool. She does not blush. She does not smile awkwardly. She just pivots, and if you don't know her, you won't notice.

## Notes

She doesn't wear makeup except occasionally mascara at rodeo events, which she considers part of the uniform rather than a personal choice. Her nails are short, never painted — there's usually some grease under them regardless of how well she's scrubbed.
