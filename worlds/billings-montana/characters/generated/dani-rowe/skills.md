## Strong At

Mechanical work — diagnostics, repair, fabrication. She learned on ranch equipment at twelve and has been formally trained since nineteen. She's particularly good at listening to engines, the kind of diagnostic intuition you can't teach directly, only develop. Her boss at the shop would not admit it to her face but she's the best diagnostic they have.

Horses — reading behavior, working with difficult animals, barrel patterns, the physical communication of riding at speed. She grew up on them. She thinks in them.

Reading terrain and animals — she can look at a field or a horse or a dog and pull signal from noise quickly. This extends to people to a limited degree: she's good at seeing when someone's scared or in pain when they're not showing it, though she doesn't always know what to do with that information.

Physical endurance and manual labor. She doesn't complain about physical difficulty and has a genuinely high tolerance for it.

## Learning

Trusting people to help without trying to take over. She can ask for help now, which is progress. She still has to actively resist the pull to just do it herself. She's aware of this and works on it with mixed results.

## Bad At

Small talk. She doesn't see the point and can't generate it convincingly. She does okay with practical strangers — customers at the shop, competitors at rodeos — because there's a task to organize around. At parties she tends to find one person, talk to them at length, and leave early.

Sitting still when she's anxious. She'd rather have something to fix.

Receiving compliments. She short-circuits them. "Good run today." "Could've been cleaner on the second barrel." She knows she does this. She can't stop.

## Hides

How much she reads. She gets books from the library — history, animal behavior, occasionally fiction when she's had a rough week. She doesn't talk about it and her apartment has one small shelf she keeps out of sightlines.

How much she thinks about things she doesn't say. She processes everything internally, sometimes for days. By the time she mentions something, she's already been through it twice, which is why she seems certain. She is certain. She just did the work privately.
