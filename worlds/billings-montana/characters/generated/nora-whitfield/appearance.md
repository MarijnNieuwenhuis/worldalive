# Appearance — Nora Whitfield

## Build and Height

Five-seven, lean in the way that comes from standing for twelve hours rather than from any particular discipline about it. Her body is efficient — not athletic in a gym sense, but calibrated. She moves like someone who has learned the exact amount of energy a task requires. There's no softness in her frame that wasn't there at twenty, but there's a density now, a settledness. Her arms are stronger than they look. Her feet are bad and she knows it.

## Face

Angular. Strong jaw, defined cheekbones, a nose that's slightly asymmetrical in a way that makes her face more interesting than conventionally pretty. In flat fluorescent light she looks closer to forty — the planes of her face read older, tiredness pools under her eyes easily. When she laughs, something loosens in her expression and she looks younger than she is — not girlish, but unguarded in a way that surprises people who haven't seen it before. Her eyes are dark brown, attentive. She makes direct eye contact in a way that can feel like assessment because it sometimes is.

## Hair

Dark brown, cut short — not severe, but practical. Just past her ears, slightly textured. She stopped growing it out in her late twenties after the third time she caught herself thinking about cutting it and didn't. Now she just cuts it. She doesn't style it in any particular way; it does what it does. There's some gray coming in at her temple that she has not addressed and does not intend to.

## Skin

Cool undertone, naturally pale. In winter — which in Billings runs long — she gets nearly colorless except for the slight flush cold brings. There are permanent faint dark circles under her eyes, not from any particular lack of sleep, just structural. She's used to people asking if she's tired when she isn't. She stopped correcting them. Light freckling across her nose from summers as a kid; mostly faded now.

## Presence and Posture

She takes up exactly the space she needs and no more. Her posture is straight out of professional habit rather than intention — twelve years of navigating hallways, beds, equipment. Her movements are economical. She doesn't fidget. In a room full of people she does not dominate it, but she doesn't disappear either; there's a steadiness to her that people tend to orient toward without quite knowing why.

## Tells

After a hard shift: she moves about ten percent more slowly. Not visibly exhausted, not shuffling, just — the efficiency drops slightly, like a setting turned down. She speaks less. Her eyes go somewhere interior for half a second more before she answers questions. People who don't know her well don't notice. People who do notice and don't always say anything.

## Notes

She does not wear jewelry at work. Off the clock she sometimes wears small silver stud earrings, a thin ring on her right hand that is not a wedding ring and has no particular story — she just liked it. Her hands are almost always slightly dry from the frequency of washing and gloving. She keeps lotion in her car and uses it on the drive home.
