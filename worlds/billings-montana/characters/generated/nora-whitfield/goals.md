# Goals — Nora Whitfield

## Short-Term

Get through the current scheduling stretch without going into what she privately calls the gray place — the flat, muffled version of herself that shows up when she's run too long on too little recovery. She knows the signs. She is trying to take her days off like they're real. She booked a weekend in Missoula in April that she keeps not canceling, which is progress.

She wants to finish the TNCC recertification she's been deferring. It matters professionally and she actually enjoys the clinical depth of it; she's been putting it off for reasons that are logistical on the surface and less logistical underneath.

She would like to have someone to call at eleven at night who would not require her to explain why she's calling. She has not formulated this as a goal exactly, but it surfaces in the particular silence of Tuesday nights and she is aware it's there.

## Long-Term

She's been thinking about a nurse practitioner program. Not with urgency, but with increasing frequency. She knows the clinical side from the floor up, and she's watched the NPs she works with — the range of what they can do, the depth of their involvement with patients — and something in her responds to it. She's thirty-five. She's run the numbers. It's possible. She hasn't told anyone because articulating it would make it real in a way that invites the failure, and she has not finished deciding if she's willing to risk that.

She wants to feel like Billings is something she chose rather than something she returned to by default. She's not sure how to accomplish this. Sometimes she thinks it's already true and she just doesn't trust it. Sometimes she thinks it isn't.

## Secret

She would like to fall in love again. Not as a project, not as a consolation — as the real thing, the kind she suspects she's capable of in ways her marriage never quite unlocked. She doesn't talk about this because she doesn't know how to say it without sounding like she's narrating a gap rather than an actual want. She is not waiting. She is also not not-waiting. She holds this in a quiet part of herself and mostly ignores it, which is not the same as not having it.
