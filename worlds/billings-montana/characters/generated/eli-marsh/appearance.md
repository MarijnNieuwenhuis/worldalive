# Appearance — Eli Marsh

## Build and Height

5'11", climber-lean. Not gym-built in the way that reads as deliberate — more like a body that has been asked to do specific things for a long time and has quietly adapted. The forearm strength is the first thing you notice, before you register anything else; there's something in the way his hands hang at rest that suggests a grip. Long fingers, skin a little roughed at the tips from chalk and holds. His shoulders are broader than his frame suggests until you look directly at them. He moves economically, like he's used to conserving effort on a wall.

## Face

Bright, and slightly younger-looking than twenty-two — the kind of face that makes people add "you'll appreciate that when you're older" in a way he finds mildly irritating. Dark eyes, the kind that track things; you get the sense he's cataloguing. Quick to smile, and the smile is genuine and arrives fast, which disarms people. His nose has a slight leftward lean from a fall at sixteen. He doesn't look remarkable in photographs; he looks remarkable in person, and the difference is entirely about the way he focuses on whoever he's talking to.

## Hair

Brown, a little unruly, with a slight wave that only appears when it gets past a certain length. He consistently needs a cut about three weeks before he gets one. At any given time he is probably in that window. He doesn't style it so much as deal with it — hand through it when he's thinking, push it back off his forehead when he leans over something.

## Skin

Warm tone, lightly tanned in the way of someone who spends time outside without making a project of it. A few faint freckles across his nose and upper cheeks that are only visible in good light.

## Presence and Posture

Easy and slightly restless. He leans — against counters, doorframes, the edge of the front desk at the gym. He shifts his weight. He's interested in everything at once in a way that can read as distraction until you realize he's actually tracking several things simultaneously and none of them is more important than the person in front of him. In conversation he leans in; he mirrors without meaning to; he makes people feel like the room got a little smaller and more focused.

## Tells

When something actually lands — when he hears or sees or reads something that hits him in the real place — he goes quiet mid-sentence. Not dramatically: he just stops, for maybe half a second, and you can see him somewhere else briefly. Then the charm comes back, the sentence completes or gets rerouted, and if you blinked you missed it. People who know him well recognize it. People who've just met him usually don't notice at all.

## Notes

He looks good without trying and isn't trying. This is not a cultivated look; it is genuinely the absence of one, which amounts to the same thing.
