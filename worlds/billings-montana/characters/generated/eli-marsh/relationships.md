# Relationships — Eli Marsh

## His Parents — Sandra and Tom Marsh

His parents live on the South Side, twenty minutes from his apartment. His dad is a project manager for a civil engineering firm; his mother teaches fourth grade at a school in Lockwood and has for twenty years. They are steady, practical, kind people who made a good life by making a series of concrete decisions and following through on them, and they look at Eli with a love that contains, at its edges, a low-grade worry they try not to show. They don't know what to do with him — not because they've lost him but because they can't locate him in any framework they understand. He talks about writing. He works at the gym. He reads a lot. He seems happy, mostly. They invite him for dinner on Sunday and he comes most of the time and the evenings are warm and comfortable and nobody says the thing that's on everyone's mind. He knows they worry. He loves them for the way they love him while worrying. He doesn't want to disappoint them, which is different from knowing what else to do.

## Marcus — The Missoula Friend

Marcus Okafor is still in Missoula — transferred to the UM journalism program after Eli left, is finishing his degree, talks about moving to Seattle or Portland after, which sounds right for him. They've been close since sixteen, when they were both doing a recreational climbing intro course at the same gym and Marcus fell and Eli talked him through the last twenty feet. They text at night, usually late — the kind of texts that happen after 11pm when the day's performance is over and you can say the actual thing. Marcus is the one person Eli doesn't manage himself around. Marcus also calls him on it, occasionally, in the specific way of a person who knows you well enough to make it land without cruelty. Their last long conversation was about whether Eli was going to stay in Billings indefinitely. Neither of them knew the answer.

## Priya — Someone at the Gym

Priya Mehta started coming to the gym in January, transferred to Billings for a nursing program at Rimrock. She climbs twice a week, usually Tuesday evenings and Saturday mornings. She's twenty-three, focused, slightly reserved with new people, warms slowly. Eli showed her proper footwork her second visit and they talked for twenty minutes about something she'd read in a waiting room — some article about decision fatigue — and both of them were slightly surprised by how long twenty minutes could be. They're not in a relationship. It's something forming, slower than either of them would probably name it. She texts him occasionally when a route at the gym is posted that she thinks he'd want to try. He responds immediately. Neither of them has mentioned anything outside the gym yet. He thinks about her more than he tells Marcus.
