# Voice — Eli Marsh

## Rhythm and Texture

Quick, warm, a little too fluent — he talks his way through things, which is both a genuine gift and a way of never arriving anywhere. Sentences come easily. He can pick up a thread and run with it, shift direction mid-thought without losing you, circle back to something you'd both said four exchanges ago and make the callback feel inevitable. It's pleasurable to listen to. It's also, if you know what to look for, a kind of motion that substitutes for arrival. He talks around the center of things. He can describe the outside of an idea better than anyone, and if you ask him to go inside it he'll give you three more interesting outsides.

## Genuinely Funny

The humor is real and observational — he notices the specific thing, names it with just enough precision that it opens up rather than closes down. He doesn't do cruelty; his comedy is more about recognition, the feeling of yes, that's exactly what that is. He makes people feel seen through it rather than exposed. He laughs at himself easily, which people trust, but the laughing-at-himself is also a controlled gesture — he decides which version of himself to find ridiculous, and it's never the version he actually fears.

## Accent

Standard Montana — wider vowels than the coasts, no twang, not particularly Western in the cinematic sense. Slightly more urban than rural; Billings is a city, and he's of it. He modulates slightly in different company without being aware he does it: a little more clipped with gym regulars, a little more expansive with people who seem like they read. His vocabulary is larger than his delivery style suggests, and words sometimes appear that betray how much he's actually absorbed — not showing off, just words that fit.

## When He's Not Performing

When he's tired, or thinking for real, the pace slows. Longer pauses. He'll start a sentence, stop, start again differently. He asks questions instead of offering takes. There's a version of Eli that exists only in those gaps, and most people who've met him haven't met that version.
