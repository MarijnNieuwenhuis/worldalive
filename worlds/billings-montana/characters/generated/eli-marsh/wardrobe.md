# Wardrobe — Eli Marsh

## Overall Style

Effortlessly casual — the kind of person who looks put-together without having made any project of it. He's not stylish in the deliberate sense; he just has a reliable baseline that happens to work. Most of his clothes are secondhand or old enough to have stopped looking new, which is its own kind of coherence. He doesn't think about his appearance much and the result looks like thoughtful restraint.

## Work (Climbing Gym)

The gym shirt — a faded heather-gray crewneck with the gym's logo on the chest, worn to the point where the logo is partly peeling. He has two; they rotate. Shorts with enough length to be practical and enough wear to look like he's actually done things in them. Approach shoes — La Sportiva TX4s, a few seasons old, resoled once. Chalk on his hands that he's stopped fully noticing. He keeps a light fleece at the desk for the early morning shifts when the building is cold.

## Casual

Worn-in jeans (one dark pair, one light that's mostly gray now), plain t-shirts in navy and white and olive, a flannel he's had since high school that somehow still fits. A brown canvas jacket he bought at a Goodwill in Missoula that is the best piece of clothing he owns. Beat-up white sneakers. He repeats combinations without noticing.

## Going Out

Same canvas jacket. A better t-shirt — one of three he doesn't wear to the gym. The dark jeans. He cleans the approach shoes or pulls out a pair of low-top boots he keeps for occasions he might describe as "slightly more adult." He looks, on these occasions, exactly like himself but cleaner.

## Won't Wear

Anything that reads as trying — no branded streetwear, no deliberate "outdoorsy" branding, no collared shirts unless someone is getting married. He's not opposed to those things philosophically; they just aren't him and wearing them would feel like a costume.

## Hands

The real tell in his appearance. Climber's hands — strong through the forearms, chalky even when he's been off the wall for hours, skin a little rough at the fingertips and the pads below the first knuckles. He has one small scar on his right palm from a crimp that broke. He uses them when he talks; they're expressive.

## Vulnerable / At Home

Basketball shorts and an old University of Montana t-shirt he technically shouldn't still wear after dropping out but does because it's soft. Socks, always. He runs cold in the evenings. He reads in this. He writes in this, on the nights he writes.
