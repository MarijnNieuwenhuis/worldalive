# Skills — Eli Marsh

## Strong At

**Observation.** The real kind — not just noticing but categorizing correctly, understanding the emotional logic underneath a surface event. He can watch a stranger at a coffee shop for two minutes and tell you something true about them, not a cold read, more like a translation. He doesn't show this off; it leaks into how he talks.

**Conversation.** He can talk to almost anyone. Not because he's performing interest but because the interest is real and people can feel the difference. He's good in the gap — the moment in a conversation where it could go quiet or go somewhere real, he tends to take it somewhere real. He asks the question that opens the door. People tell him things. He remembers what he's told.

**Climbing.** Technically solid, particularly on technical face routes where reading the wall matters more than brute strength. He's been climbing since fifteen. He's not elite but he's good — he moves on rock with the casual economy of someone who has spent enough time up there to stop being afraid of it. He's a better teacher than he'd say; his gym regulars request him.

**Making people feel comfortable.** Rapid, genuine, not calculated. Walk-in customers at the gym, strangers at parties, people who are obviously nervous — he settles them. He doesn't know exactly how. He thinks it might be that he's visibly curious about them and not visibly performing anything, and people can tell.

## Learning

Finishing things. Sitting still with discomfort instead of moving around it. Asking the question that's actually on his mind rather than the adjacent interesting question.

## Bad At

Commitment — not in the melodramatic sense but in the sustained, daily sense of choosing something and continuing to choose it after the novelty is gone. He keeps the gym job because the gym asks nothing of him beyond his body and his social ease. He doesn't keep the harder things. He starts a new notebook section every few weeks instead of filling the old one.

Asking himself the hard questions. He's exceptional at asking other people the real questions — the ones that open doors. He asks himself interesting questions, which is different. Interesting questions have no wrong answers. Hard questions have real ones.

## What He Hides

How much he reads. How much he cares — about the work, about doing something real, about the possibility that he matters. How scared he is. He hides the fear behind the charm and the curiosity and the easy talk, and it works on almost everyone. It works on him most of the time too.
