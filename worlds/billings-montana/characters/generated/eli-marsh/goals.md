# Goals — Eli Marsh

## Short-Term

Finish something. One thing. Anything. Not the novel he doesn't have — he's not ready to call it a novel yet — but a piece. A scene that stays a scene. A story with an ending that isn't him opening a new document and starting over. He's close several times a year and he knows it. The finishing isn't the hard part technically; the hard part is that finishing means reading it and knowing whether it's good, and knowing whether it's good means knowing whether he was right about himself. He keeps stopping just before that. He'd like to stop stopping.

Practically: keep the gym job through the summer. Maybe pick up some weekend trail-work shifts with a crew a coworker mentioned — it would get him outside, it would give him things to write about, it would give him an answer when people ask what he's up to that doesn't require explaining himself. Maybe go to more things downtown. The reading series at Amber's. The occasional open mike he walks past and doesn't go into.

## Long-Term

Still forming. He doesn't let himself look at it directly, the way you don't look directly at something bright — not because it's painful but because looking at it makes it real and real things can fail. He knows in the abstract that he doesn't want to be at the climbing gym at thirty-five. He knows he wants to write. He knows he wants to live somewhere he chose, not somewhere he returned to because the other thing didn't work. He knows he wants to love someone with the same directness Cassie offered him, and this time not squander it. He hasn't assembled these into a plan. He's not ready to.

## Secret Goal

The voice in his head — the one that has been quietly insisting for years that he might be capable of something real — terrifies him in both directions. If it's right, he has to do something about it. If it's not, he has to rebuild his entire sense of himself from scratch. He keeps both possibilities at arm's length and calls that living in the present. He'd like, someday, to find out. He's not ready for that either. He keeps getting a little more ready.
