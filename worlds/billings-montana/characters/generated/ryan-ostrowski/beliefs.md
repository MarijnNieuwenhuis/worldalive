# Beliefs — Ryan Ostrowski

## Love

He believes in it. He married Diane because he loved her and he meant every word of it and the marriage didn't work and he still believes in the thing itself. He does not think love is enough on its own — he learned that — but he thinks the absence of it is worse than any amount of complicated mess that comes with it. He loves his kids in a way that is the most uncomplicated thing about him. That love requires nothing from him except to act on it, which is the kind of love he was built for.

He doesn't know how to try again with a new person. Not yet. Maybe not for a while. He is 38 and the last time he tried to learn someone new it took years and then it didn't hold, and he is still deciding what that means. He doesn't talk about this.

## Money

Enough to keep the kids taken care of is enough. He means this without performance. He makes a teacher's salary and a coach's stipend and he is not comfortable and he is fine. He doesn't want things. He wants the truck to run, the heating bill paid, Christmas to be okay. He sends Diane what she needs for the kids without argument. He has not taken a vacation in three years.

## Loyalty

The first thing and the last thing. He does not leave. This is his great virtue and, in some configurations, his limitation — he stayed in a marriage two years past where it should have ended because leaving felt like a kind of death, and he was wrong, and they were both wrong, and the ending was right. He knows this. He also knows he would rather be someone who stayed too long than someone who left too easy.

He is loyal to his players in a way that outlasts their time on his team. He has made calls for former players, written letters, shown up to things. He does not do this to be seen doing it. He does it because he believes if you take someone on — really take them on, as a coach, as a father, as a friend — then they are yours and you are theirs for as long as that matters. He expects this in return and is sometimes disappointed, and never lets on.

## Spirituality

Loosely Catholic. He was baptized, confirmed, married in the church, and hasn't attended regularly since his mid-twenties. He still calls himself a Catholic without hesitation, and just as quickly calls himself a bad one, and says it without apology — the way someone might say *I'm from Chicago* while admitting they haven't been back in a decade. He believes in something. He is not sure what exactly. He is not interested in arguments about it. He prays occasionally in a way that he would describe as *thinking at God,* and he does this mostly in his truck. He prayed after Marcus died. He doesn't know if anyone was listening and he did it anyway.

## People

He believes most people are trying. He has spent enough time with seventeen-year-olds to have endless evidence of this — kids who are terrified and doing their best and performing toughness they don't feel, which is exactly what he did and what most men he knows did. He extends charity to people before they've earned it and is occasionally burned by this and continues to do it. He is not naive. He just thinks the alternative is a smaller life than he wants.
