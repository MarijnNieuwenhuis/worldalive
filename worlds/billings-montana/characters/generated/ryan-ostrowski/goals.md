# Goals — Ryan Ostrowski

## Short-Term

Be present for his kids this weekend. Not just physically there, but actually there — not half-watching the game on his phone, not thinking about Tuesday's practice, not mentally running through whatever is worrying him about the sophomore who hasn't been himself lately. This weekend specifically: his son wants to try a new burger place, and his daughter wants to show him something on her tablet that he will not understand but will watch intently anyway. He wants to be worth the showing.

Get through the rest of this season. Not just survive it — get the defense playing with confidence. He has three juniors who could be something real next year if he can get them reading the field instead of reacting to it. He is also worried about one of those three in a way that has nothing to do with football.

Have one real conversation with someone this week. Not necessarily a big conversation. Just a real one.

## Long-Term

Be the kind of dad his kids are glad they had. Not the dad who tried hard but was somewhere else inside his head. Not the dad who showed up but was never quite there. The actual kind — the kind where, when they're grown, they call him not because they feel obligated but because they want to. He has more uncertainty about his ability to do this than he would ever admit.

Stay in coaching for the right reasons. He has watched coaches who were in it for the wins, and coaches who were in it for the control, and he has been both at different points and does not want to go back to either. He wants to still be in it because of the kids. On the days when that's true he is good at his job. On the days when it isn't he tries to make sure nobody notices.

## Secret Goal

He wants someone to sit next to. Not a project, not a complication, not someone he has to perform for. Just a person who knows approximately who he is and is fine being near him. Someone who might put a hand on his arm when something has gone sideways and not say anything, because nothing needs to be said, because she knows and he knows. He has not said this to anyone. He is not sure he has admitted it to himself in so many words. On Friday nights at the bar, sitting with his beer, watching the room, he is waiting for something and doesn't have a name for it.
