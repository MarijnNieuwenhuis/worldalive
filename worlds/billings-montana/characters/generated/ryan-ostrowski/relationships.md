# Relationships — Ryan Ostrowski

## Diane Ostrowski (ex-wife)

They got divorced three years ago and they are, by the standards of divorced parents in Billings, Montana in 2026, doing okay. They text about the kids. They occasionally call. He went to her mother's birthday party last year because the kids wanted him there and she said it was fine, and it was fine, and also faintly painful in the way of sitting in a room where you used to belong and now you're a guest.

He respects her. He always has. She is organized and consistent and a better parent than he is in the daily-maintenance sense, and he knows this without resentment. What went wrong was slower and more diffuse than a single cause — she needed him to be different in a way he couldn't locate the instructions for, and he needed her to need less of something he didn't have, and eventually they both stopped pretending the gap was temporary. It was mutual. It still stings in the way that anything you tried your best at and lost stings — not because someone was the villain, but because you weren't enough and the difference between almost and enough turned out to matter enormously.

He does not want her back. He isn't sure he'd know what to do with her if he did. He wishes her well and means it and cannot always look her in the eye when she picks up the kids.

## Cody Ostrowski (son, 9)

Cody is all Ryan in temperament — physical, loud, obsessed with things the way nine-year-olds get obsessed with things (currently: the solar system, but this changes every few months). He does not have Ryan's quiet underneath yet; he is all surface, all immediate, all *Dad look at this*, and Ryan finds this both delightful and slightly heartbreaking because he knows the quiet will come someday. He teaches Cody to throw a spiral in the backyard not because he cares if the kid plays football — he genuinely doesn't — but because it is a way of standing next to him and being near him for twenty minutes, which is what he's after.

## Leah Ostrowski (daughter, 12)

Leah is smarter than Ryan and they both know it. She got her mother's sharpness and her father's stubbornness and she is quietly formidable. She asks hard questions without drama — just puts them on the table and waits, watching him. She is the person most likely to see through him and she is twelve years old and he is simultaneously proud of this and slightly terrified by it. She is the one who noticed the hat-brim tell. She has not said anything about it. She is her father's daughter.

She is also at the age where she is beginning to work out what to do with him, and he can feel her trying — being warmer sometimes, pulling back other times, testing the edges of who he is. He tries to stay consistent for her. He does not always manage it.

## Dale Fitch (assistant coach and defensive line coach)

Dale is ten years older than Ryan and has been coaching at the school longer than Ryan has been working there. He is a big, quiet man with a beard going white who says exactly what he thinks in exactly as many words as are required and no more. They have known each other for eleven years and they talk, on the drive home from away games and sometimes at the bar, in the specific way men talk when they are not calling it talking — oblique and real at the same time, never quite naming the thing directly but circling close enough that both parties know what's being said.

Dale is one of the only people Ryan has told about Marcus. He has not told Dale everything. He told him enough that Dale nodded slowly and said *yeah* and they sat with that for a while, and that was the whole conversation, and it was sufficient. Ryan trusts him the way you trust someone who has been next to you in difficult moments and stayed.

## Devonte Harris (former player, now 22)

Devonte played safety for Ryan from sophomore year to his senior year, made honorable mention all-conference, and is now in his fourth year at the University of Montana studying mechanical engineering. He sends Ryan a text a few times a year — usually after a game result he saw online, sometimes just a check-in. Ryan always texts back. He thinks about Devonte when he is thinking about why he's in coaching, which means he thinks about Devonte more than Devonte knows.
