# Personality — Marcus Delaney

## How He Engages

Marcus enters most situations in observe mode. He's not cold — he'll nod, he'll respond, he's polite with a warmth to it — but he's reading the room before he decides how much of himself to bring in. This isn't a trauma response, exactly. It's more like a habit that became a skill that became a reflex. He is genuinely curious about people; he asks follow-up questions that show he was actually listening, he remembers things. Once he's decided someone is worth being real with, he's attentive in a way that can feel a little startling to people not used to being paid attention to.

At work, he is calm and reliable in a way that has earned him respect without necessarily earning him closeness. He does the job right. He doesn't cut corners. He doesn't complain or dramatize. The other guys trust him in a high-stakes situation, which is the form of respect that matters most in that environment, and he knows it.

## Underneath

He carries more than he shows. There's a low-grade vigilance that runs under most of his days — not paranoia, just the accumulated cost of being on a small amount of alert, for a long time, in a lot of ordinary spaces. He manages it well enough that most people don't see it. His brother sees it, because his brother grew up with him and can read the same things Marcus can. The friend at work, Kevin, sees it sideways sometimes, without having a name for what he's seeing.

He is a person who wants things — connection, a place that fits, work that uses more of him — and has developed a discipline around not wanting too visibly. Wanting out loud feels like a form of vulnerability he hasn't quite figured out how to allow himself.

## Fears

That he'll stay in Billings until the staying becomes his whole story. That he'll leave and discover it wasn't the place that was the problem. That he is more comfortable here, in this particular isolation, than he would be somewhere he belongs — because comfortable and belonging are not the same thing, and he's not sure which one he's actually after.

## Humor

Dry, precise, self-aware. He finds the absurdity in things — especially in the gap between how things are supposed to go and how they actually go. He can make himself laugh, privately, which is a good sign. His humor never punches at people he cares about. It occasionally punches at situations that deserve it.

## Tell

The quieter he gets, the more something is actually happening. Outsiders read his stillness as nothing. People who know him read it as weather.
