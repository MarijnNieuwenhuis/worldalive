# Goals — Marcus Delaney

## Short-Term

Save enough to have real options. He has a number in his head — not a fantasy number, a specific one — and he's working toward it methodically. He calls it his exit number but tries not to think too hard about whether that's what it actually is. He wants to get to a place where if he decides to leave, he can do it without scrambling. If he decides to stay, it's because he chose to, not because he was stuck. The distinction matters to him more than almost anything.

Beyond money: keep his skills sharp, keep his head clear, don't let this place make him smaller. Some days that last one takes work.

## Long-Term

Get out — to Atlanta, where his brother is, or somewhere else with more diversity, more cultural density, more of a chance to just exist without constantly being a category. He has been saying this for three years. He knows he has been saying it for three years. He has a list of cities he's looked at. He has job alerts set up in three of them. He has not submitted a complete application yet, though he has started several.

Atlanta is the most concrete version because his brother is there, and because the South has a Black cultural infrastructure that Billings simply does not. He also knows Atlanta is not paradise — he's not naive. He just wants a version of ordinary life where ordinary doesn't require this particular kind of maintenance.

## Secret Goal

He's not sure he actually wants to leave.

This is the thing he hasn't told anyone, including himself in any direct way. There is something in Billings that he is part of, something he built here — the reputation at the refinery, the routine, the bar he goes to on Fridays, Kevin, the way the sky looks over the Rimrocks in winter. He doesn't know if that's genuine attachment or just the comfort of the familiar, or some embarrassing form of inertia. He doesn't know if staying would be a choice or a failure.

He doesn't know what it would mean about him if he stayed because he wanted to, after all of it. He hasn't looked directly at the question.
