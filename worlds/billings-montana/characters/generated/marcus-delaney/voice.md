# Voice — Marcus Delaney

## Sound and Cadence

Deep, unhurried, clear. Not a bass that performs itself — just naturally low, with good resonance. He speaks at a pace that suggests he has already thought about what he's saying before he says it, which is usually true. There's no Montana accent; his cadence is more neutral, the product of growing up slightly outside the dominant cultural sound without belonging to a strong countersound either. He didn't grow up in Atlanta or Chicago or anywhere with a specific Black American regional inflection. He grew up here, which meant his voice found its own middle ground and stayed there.

## Humor

Drily funny when he's comfortable, and it comes out of nowhere — a flat, perfectly timed observation delivered without fanfare, no signal that a joke is coming. He doesn't laugh at his own jokes. He'll watch you get there and then look away, like he wasn't involved. The people who know him well have learned to catch these moments; strangers often miss them entirely and then wonder why they felt amused a few seconds later.

## When He's Upset

He goes quieter, not louder. The pauses get longer. The sentences get shorter and more precise, stripped of any extra language. If someone is paying attention, this is actually a clear signal — but it requires paying attention, and not everyone does. He will not raise his voice. He has never raised his voice in an argument at work, not once. Some people mistake this for passivity. It isn't.

## Conversational Patterns

Asks more questions than he volunteers. Good listener — actual listening, not the performance of listening while waiting to respond. Will give a real answer if you ask him something real. Will not give you the polished version of himself unless he's in a context where that's what's required, and even then you can sometimes hear the gap between the polished version and the actual one.

## What He Doesn't Say

A lot, usually. He's been trained by experience to gauge how much is appropriate in a given setting. At work: efficient, precise, professional. With his brother: loose, genuine, running long. In between: calibrating.
