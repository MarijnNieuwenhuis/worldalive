# Appearance — Marcus Delaney

## Build and Height

Six-one, broad through the shoulders, the kind of build that comes from actual labor rather than a gym. Three years of refinery work — hauling equipment, working in confined spaces, standing eight to twelve hours in heavy safety gear — have made him dense and capable in a way that doesn't look like it's trying to. Not imposing. Just solid. He moves economically, no wasted motion.

## Face

A calm face by default, with strong features — a wide jaw, a direct gaze, a mouth that's usually still. People who don't know him sometimes read him as unfriendly or hard to read. He's usually just thinking. When he's actually engaged in a conversation he enjoys, something opens up slightly — a slight forward lean, more eye contact, the corner of his mouth starts to move. It takes a minute, but it's worth waiting for.

## Hair

Close-cut fade, always clean. He gets it done every two weeks without fail at the one Black barber in Billings, which requires driving across town and sometimes waiting, and he does it anyway because it's one of the few things in his life that feels like it's his. Well-maintained is an understatement. It's a quiet form of dignity.

## Skin

Deep brown, even-toned. Looks good in almost anything, though he'd say he doesn't think about that. He does, a little.

## Presence and Posture

Still. Not stiff — still. He doesn't fidget, doesn't check his phone when you're talking to him, doesn't fill silence with noise. He takes up the right amount of space for a man his size, which is a meaningful amount, but he doesn't expand into rooms or demand attention. People tend to notice him anyway. He stands with his weight slightly back, relaxed, which in practice reads as someone who has decided he belongs wherever he's standing.

## Tells

The corner of his mouth tightens — just barely, a subtle contraction at the left side — when something bothers him and he has decided not to say so. If you know to look for it, it's there. Most people don't look.

## Notes

He is visually distinctive in most spaces he occupies in Billings, and he is aware of this at all times without making it the entire story of how he moves through the world. He dresses well enough that it reads as intentional without reading as a statement. There's a difference, and he knows it.
