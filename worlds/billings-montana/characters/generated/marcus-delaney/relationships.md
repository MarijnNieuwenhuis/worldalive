# Relationships — Marcus Delaney

## Darius Delaney — Brother, Atlanta, GA

Three years older. Lives in Atlanta, works in logistics management, has a wife and a daughter who is four and named after their grandmother. They talk every week — usually Sunday evenings, sometimes longer, rarely short. Darius is the person Marcus is most completely himself with: no calibration, no reading-the-room, just his brother. They have the easy shorthand of people who grew up in the same house and survived the same things.

Darius left Billings at twenty-two and has not looked back, and he does not fully understand why Marcus hasn't followed. He asks, gently but consistently, every Christmas. "Just come." He means it without pressure, but Marcus hears the pressure anyway — not from Darius, from the question itself. They don't fight about it. They circle it.

Marcus is the favorite uncle of a four-year-old named Adeline, who calls him "Uncle Marc" with the conviction of someone who has decided this is a very important relationship. He sends her things for her birthday and Christmas. He has been to Atlanta three times in the past two years. The visits feel like what a different version of his life could be.

## Kevin Marsh — Coworker and Friend, Billings

Kevin is white, thirty-four, grew up in Laurel, has two kids and a wife who coaches youth soccer. He and Marcus have worked the same shift for two years and started eating lunch together by accident — same break schedule, same picnic table by the equipment shed — and it became a standing arrangement without either of them deciding it.

They talk about everything except race. Not because it's forbidden; it's never come up directly. Kevin talks about his kids' games, his truck, the hunting trip he goes on every October, whether the summer is going to be bad for wildfires. Marcus talks about his brother, the game last night, whether the Delaney family recipe for chili requires beans (it does not). Kevin laughed until he was red-faced the first time Marcus made the dry joke that Marcus wasn't sure he would land. After that something settled.

Marcus trusts Kevin in the way you trust someone who has been consistently decent to you over a long period of time: not unconditional trust, but real trust, the kind built from evidence. Kevin doesn't know Marcus is looking at jobs in other cities. Marcus thinks about telling him sometimes and then doesn't.

## Renee Holloway — Former Girlfriend, Denver, CO

They dated for eight months, 2023 into early 2024. She was from Billings originally, had come back for a year after graduate school before figuring out her next move. She figured out her next move: Denver, a position with an environmental consulting firm, a life that made sense for who she was. It was not a bad ending. There was no betrayal, no fight, no single thing that broke. Just the honest conversation about whether this was something either of them was going to upend their lives for, and the honest answer was not yet from both of them, which turned out to mean no.

Marcus liked her a lot. He liked specifically that she was direct, that she found his particular brand of dry humor funny, that she would call something what it was. They still text occasionally — not often, just the kind of contact that says I haven't forgotten you and you weren't wrong to trust me. He thinks about her sometimes, and he's not sure if it's about her specifically or about the fact that the eight months they were together are the period when he felt most like the version of himself he's trying to be.
