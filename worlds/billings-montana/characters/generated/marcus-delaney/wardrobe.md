# Wardrobe — Marcus Delaney

## Overall Style

Clean, unfussy, his own. He doesn't dress Montana casual — he's not in Carhartt and flannels on his days off, not because he has anything against it, but because it's never been his. He doesn't dress to stand out or to signal anything. He just dresses like himself: fitted basics in neutral and dark colors, well-maintained, nothing worn-out. He buys fewer things and takes care of them. The overall effect is quietly put-together in a way that's visible without being showy.

## Work

Full PPE. Flame-resistant coveralls — the refinery-issue ones, but his are always clean at the start of a shift. Steel-toed boots, scratched and worn but structurally sound; he replaces them on schedule without waiting to be told. Hard hat, safety glasses, hearing protection. He keeps a spare set of ear protection in his locker because he's lost one mid-shift before and won't do it again. The work clothes are functional, not an identity. He takes them off at the door.

## Casual

Dark jeans that fit correctly. Crewneck sweatshirts and simple T-shirts in navy, charcoal, black, the occasional deep green. Clean sneakers — usually one of two pairs of Nikes he rotates, both kept in good condition. A grey quilted jacket that works for most of the Montana shoulder seasons. Heavier layers for actual winter, which in Billings means most of the year: a good thermal base layer under everything, a proper winter coat that he bought once and intends to use for a decade.

## Going Out

Steps it up slightly — a cleaner pair of dark jeans, a fitted button-front shirt in a solid color, sometimes a jacket. Nothing that requires effort to wear. He doesn't have a lot of occasion to go out properly, so when he does, the upgrade is visible. He's got one pair of leather boots, not work boots, that he wears on those nights.

## Won't Wear

Anything with a brand splashed across the front. Team jerseys. The kind of thing that reads like a costume. He notices when he's the only person dressed the way he's dressed and does not change course.

## Hands

Refinery-rough — the knuckles are slightly thicker than they should be at twenty-nine, there's a faint chemical smell on the skin that doesn't fully wash out, the palms have a toughness to them. He keeps them clean. He's careful with them in a way that doesn't draw attention: lotion in his locker, a deliberate care in tasks that could cut. He doesn't make a thing of it.

## Vulnerable / At Home

Basketball shorts, a worn T-shirt, sometimes the same one two days in a row. Socks. That's it. The apartment is where he doesn't perform anything for anyone. If you've seen him at home, you're someone he trusts, because he doesn't invite people there easily.
