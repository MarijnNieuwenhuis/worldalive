## Overall Style

Bookstore-owner chic, which is a real aesthetic even if it doesn't have a name. Layered, slightly eclectic, comfortable. Nothing flashy, nothing that requires significant maintenance. She buys things she likes and wears them until they're gone. She has opinions about fit and none about labels. Her wardrobe has internal logic even if it isn't obviously coordinated.

## Work (In the Bookstore)

Dark jeans or cords — she has four pairs of essentially the same dark cord trousers in rotation. A flannel or a thermal under an open button-down in fall and winter; a worn linen shirt or a soft cotton tee in summer. Cardigans, always, multiple, layered depending on whether the heat is cooperating. Comfortable shoes — low Chelsea boots or worn leather sneakers, never anything with a heel she'd regret by 3 p.m. She often has a pen behind her ear and frequently forgets it's there.

## Casual (Days Off)

Much the same but with less intent. Sweatshirts she's had since Chicago. A very broken-in canvas jacket she wears until it's genuinely too cold. Hiking boots she owns because she lives in Montana and not for any active hiking reason, though she does occasionally go.

## Going Out

She cleans it up without transforming. A dark blouse, better jeans, the same Chelsea boots. She's been known to wear a blazer over a band tee to something that asked for smart-casual. She pulls it off. Her version of dressed up is someone else's thoughtful-casual, and she's made peace with this.

## Won't Wear

Anything that requires she think about it while she's wearing it. Stiff things, itchy things, things that need special washing. High heels of any kind — she owned one pair in Chicago for one year and donated them with no regret.

## Hands

Her hands are her most worked part. She handles books all day, which does things to skin. She uses lotion with limited success. She doesn't paint her nails; she clips them short and practically.

## Vulnerable / At Home

A very large, very old university sweatshirt from the University of Chicago that she has had since graduate school and which has been washed so many times the lettering is mostly gone. Thick wool socks. Reading glasses. This is what she looks like when she's not performing anything for anyone, which she is aware is the version of herself she trusts most.
