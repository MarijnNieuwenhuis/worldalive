## Short-Term

Keep the bookstore alive. This sounds modest and is not. Independent bookselling in a mid-sized western city in 2026 is an act of sustained will. She does events — author readings, a monthly philosophy book club she runs herself, a children's story hour on Saturdays that she started reluctantly and now defends with ferocity. She's working on a relationship with a local high school to get students in for curriculum books. She is exploring whether there's a way to do a small press imprint without losing her mind or her savings. Every week she runs the numbers. Every week she decides to keep going.

## Long-Term

Write something. She doesn't say what to anyone. She's not sure yet what it is — not the dissertation, not exactly, though it would draw from it. Something that uses the years of thinking she did and the years since. Something that takes Billings seriously as a place worth thinking about, and philosophy seriously as a practice worth explaining to people who think it's only for seminars. She hasn't started. She opens a blank document occasionally and closes it. She is still gathering herself toward it.

## Secret

To find out if she still has the mind she used to have — the one that her professors flagged in letters of recommendation, the one that got her into the program at Chicago on partial funding with a note about her potential. She doesn't know if it went anywhere or just went quiet. She needs to know. Not for anyone else. For herself, to be finished with the question one way or the other.
