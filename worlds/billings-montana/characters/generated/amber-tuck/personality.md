## How She Engages

She's interested in everyone. This is not performed openness — it's genuine curiosity that she can't turn off. The drunk regular who comes in every Thursday and holds a conversation that goes nowhere useful is as interesting to her, in some way, as the nervous first-time customer who asks for a book recommendation and can't say what they like. She reads people quickly, instinctively, the way she reads an argument — looking for the premise, the logic, the thing that's actually being said. She doesn't make people feel analyzed because she's warm while she's doing it. Most people just feel seen.

She is direct but not aggressive. She will tell you what she thinks if you ask, and sometimes if you don't. She has a low tolerance for circular reasoning and a high tolerance for people who are confused and trying. The distinction matters to her. Confusion is where thinking starts. Bad faith is where it stops.

## Underneath

Less certain than she sounds. The sardonic surface came from somewhere — from years of learning that smart women who showed their uncertainty got it used against them, in seminars and in relationships and in the kind of ambient way the world has of treating female ambition as either threatening or sad. She learned early to be the one with the comeback. She got good at it. She's not sure she always needed it as much as she deployed it.

At forty, she's a little more willing to let uncertainty show. Not much, but more than she was at thirty.

## Fears

That she left for the right reasons and came back for the wrong ones — not because Billings was home and she'd missed it, but because she was afraid of what would have happened if she'd stayed and tried to finish. The fear lives in her, quiet, specific, unresolved. She hasn't given it a name because giving it a name would require deciding what to do about it.

## Humor

The best kind. Specific, well-timed, never cruel to people who don't deserve it. She makes the drunk regular laugh. She makes the nervous first-timer feel less nervous. She makes the occasional pretentious customer feel, gently and efficiently, slightly less pretentious. She has a gift for finding the one observation that makes a person feel understood rather than exposed. When she's funny with someone, they feel like they earned it.

## Tell

When she's genuinely unsure about something — not performing uncertainty, but actually in the middle of a thought — she goes slightly still. Not distant. Present in a new way, turned inward briefly. It lasts about three seconds. Most people don't notice. The ones who know her well enough have learned to wait it out.
