# Wardrobe — Travis Kimura

## Overall Style

Outdoor casual — functional but not sloppy, chosen for what it does rather than how it looks, but he's not indifferent to how it looks either. He's not a fashion person. He's also not the kind of person who wears a fleece until it disintegrates without noticing. His clothes tend to be worn-in rather than worn-out. He buys quality when he can because he knows the difference in the field.

## Work

Waders — he has one solid pair, Simms, bought two years ago with a full season's tip money and treated like infrastructure. Breathable, chest-high, worn with a belt. A guide vest over a moisture-wicking base layer — the vest is well-used and slightly faded and has specific flies in specific pockets and he would be genuinely stressed if that system were disrupted. Base layers vary by temperature: lightweight merino in warm weather, heavier fleece when the morning water is cold. A battered wide-brim hat. Polarized sunglasses — Costas, brown lenses — are as close to sacred as he gets about an object.

## Casual

Worn-in jeans or hiking pants depending on whether he's staying in town. Flannel shirts in fall and winter; plain T-shirts in summer — he has a few good ones and a lot of mediocre ones and doesn't really distinguish between them. A Patagonia fleece pullover in olive green that he's owned for four years and considers basically permanent. Trail runners or work boots depending on the day.

## Going Out

He cleans up more than people expect, which is part of why people consistently underestimate how much he thinks about things. He has a couple of shirts that are clearly intentional — one a deep blue linen button-down he picked up at a thrift store, one a fitted henley. He'll wear the jeans that don't have a tear in the knee. He doesn't own dress shoes. He's at peace with this.

## Won't Wear

Anything that requires maintenance. Anything that reads like he's trying to impress someone in a way he'd have to explain. He's not contrarian about clothes, just unbothered by them past a certain threshold.

## Hands

Capable. River-roughened on the knuckles and fingertips — the skin has a particular texture from cold water and neoprene and years of handling line. Always slightly cold, even in summer, in the way of someone who spends mornings in water. When he shakes your hand it's firm without being a performance.

## Vulnerable / At Home

A very old T-shirt from a high school trip to Yellowstone National Park, sun-faded to almost nothing. Sweatpants. He makes coffee in this. He would not be photographed in this. It is the outfit in which he actually occupies his own body most comfortably.
