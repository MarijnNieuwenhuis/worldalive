# Relationships — Travis Kimura

## His Mother — Yuki Kimura (née Tanaka)

She came from Osaka at 22, met his father within a year, and has been here ever since — thirty years in Montana, and she still watches the landscape like she's not entirely sure it's real. Travis loves her in the specific way of someone who grew up watching a person be brave in a country that wasn't theirs. She kept her Japanese and gave it to him in pieces — not full fluency, more like a handful of words and phrases that are more hers than English, that belong to the specific emotional register she doesn't have in translation. They talk in a mix. "Daijobu," she'll say, and mean more than okay. He says it back sometimes when he means more than okay.

She worries about him in a way she mostly keeps to herself. She knows he runs too warm toward everyone else and not warm enough toward himself — she sees the pattern because she has her own version of it. They don't talk about this directly. They talk about the river, about what he's reading, about her garden. The love is in the talking about other things.

## Cody Brecht — Best Friend Since High School

They met in ninth grade, have remained friends without drama or deliberate effort — one of those friendships that persists because neither person sees a reason to stop. Cody works in construction, lives on the south side, has a girlfriend Travis likes fine and a dog Travis likes better. They hang out at each other's places, watch games they're mildly invested in, go out occasionally but not strenuously. They don't do deep emotional processing with each other and never have, and Travis finds this genuinely restful — he can be around Cody without monitoring anything. Cody thinks Travis is the most together person he knows. Travis has never corrected this.

## Dale Rourke — Repeat Client, Something More Like a Friend

Dale is 51, retired early from something in tech, lives in Denver but comes up to fish the Yellowstone two or three times a year and specifically requests Travis when he books. They've spent maybe twenty days total on the water together, which is a strange kind of intimacy — you learn a lot about a person in waders in moving water for eight hours. Dale is one of the few people who's asked Travis real questions and waited through the redirects for the actual answer. Travis hasn't fully let him in, but he's let him further in than most. Dale noticed the wrist thing once and didn't say anything, just changed the subject gently and let Travis land. Travis noticed that Dale noticed. He hasn't mentioned it. He thinks about it more than he'd expect.
