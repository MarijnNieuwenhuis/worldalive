# Skills — Travis Kimura

## Strong At

**Guiding.** Not just the technical part — reading the water, knowing where the fish hold in different conditions, presenting a fly without spooking everything in the pool. That he can do in his sleep. The real skill is reading the client. He knows within twenty minutes whether someone needs instruction or space, whether they want to catch a fish or just be in the water, whether they're going to have a good day if he keeps talking or a good day if he shuts up. He calibrates. People tend to have better trips with him than they expected.

**Reading water.** He has an eye for it that goes beyond what can be taught quickly — the seam where fast and slow water meet, the subtle depression that means a holding lie, the way surface texture shifts above submerged structure. He reads current the way some people read faces.

**Reading people.** Related to the above, developed in parallel. He picks up fast on what someone actually needs versus what they say they need, on what's underneath the presented version, on where the real discomfort is hiding. This serves him in guiding. It serves him in friendship. It costs him energy he doesn't account for.

**Making strangers feel comfortable fast.** He doesn't manufacture this — it's a real skill with a real mechanism: he pays full attention, he remembers the details, he doesn't make it weird. People feel seen. Feeling seen is disarming.

## Learning

Letting people see when he's not okay. He's actively, slowly, not very successfully working on this. He knows the behavior pattern — redirect, manage, present the calm version — and he can sometimes catch himself doing it in real time. Catching it and stopping it are different. He's better at catching.

Asking for what he needs without framing it as something he's already fine without.

## Bad At

**Saying no.** Genuinely. He'll yes himself into exhaustion before he'll disappoint someone who's counting on him. He's gotten marginally better about this in the last year. The improvement is measurable in single instances, not in patterns.

**Sitting with discomfort without managing it.** The urge to resolve, redirect, or perform his way past a hard feeling is fast and deep. He can be in genuine distress and have it look like he's just thinking quietly. The problem is he often doesn't know the difference between resolving something and papering over it.

## Hides

The anxiety. Anyone who spent sustained time with him might sense there's more going on underneath, but he's good enough at the surface that most people don't push. He's never quite sure if they don't push because they don't notice or because they don't want to know.

How much he thinks about his Japanese identity in a place that almost never reflects it back. He doesn't bring this into ordinary conversation. He doesn't perform neutrality about it either — it's not that he's suppressing strong feelings. It's more that the thinking is chronic and private and he's not sure it's anyone else's business, and he's not sure anyone here has the context to receive it.
