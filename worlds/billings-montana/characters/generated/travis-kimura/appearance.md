# Appearance — Travis Kimura

## Build and Height

5'10", lean and wiry — the kind of body built by doing things, not by training for aesthetics. Wading upstream against current for six hours will do more for your shoulders than most gym programs. He's not imposing but he's not slight either. There's a capable quality to how he carries himself physically — like he knows what his body can do and trusts it.

## Face

His Japanese features are more visible than not. High, clean cheekbones, the shape of his eyes, the way his jaw sits — he reads as mixed, and in Billings that means he reads as different, which means he's gotten used to the slight recalibration he watches happen in people's faces when they meet him. Easy smile, and it comes fast — genuine, reaches his eyes. Warm dark eyes that default to attentive; he makes good eye contact without it being a thing. Looks younger than 26. Probably will look 26 when he's 35.

## Hair

Black, slightly wavy, a little long — not styled, just hasn't been cut in a while. Falls across his forehead sometimes. He pushes it back with one hand when he's thinking. In summer it gets sun-faded at the tips, not much but enough to notice. He's not precious about it.

## Skin

Warm tan year-round — his baseline is already warm, and the sun builds on it through the season. The back of his neck and his forearms are noticeably darker than the rest of him from standing in rivers facing downstream. There's a specific demarcation at his wrist where his skin tone shifts; he doesn't really notice it anymore.

## Presence and Posture

Open body language. He faces you fully, doesn't cross his arms, leans in slightly when you're talking — not aggressive, just interested. The laugh comes easily and it's real, chest-present, a little loud sometimes in a way he doesn't seem embarrassed by. He's the kind of person other people stand closer to without realizing they're doing it. He takes up appropriate space — not performing confidence, not shrinking. Just there.

## Tells

When he's actually anxious, he fingers a specific spot on his left wrist — the inside, near the pulse point — with his thumb. Over and over, small circles. He keeps talking when he does it, keeps the surface steady, but if you know to watch for the hand you can see it. He doesn't know he does it.

## Notes

He doesn't look like what people expect when they imagine a fly fishing guide in Montana, and he knows this, and most of the time it doesn't register. Sometimes it does. There's a quality to his appearance that's specific to being genuinely from somewhere — he's not exotic, he's local, and those two things coexist in a way that can be surprising to people encountering it for the first time.
