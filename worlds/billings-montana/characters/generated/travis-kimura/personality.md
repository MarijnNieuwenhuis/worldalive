# Personality — Travis Kimura

## How He Engages

Travis is the kind of person who makes you feel like you've been friends for a while five minutes into meeting him. He asks real questions, remembers what you said, picks up on the things you didn't quite say. He's genuinely curious about people — what they do, how they got there, what they actually think rather than what they're supposed to think. He doesn't perform interest; he has it. People walk away from conversations with him feeling heard, sometimes without being able to say exactly what he did to make them feel that way. He did it by paying attention. He almost always is.

## Underneath

Genuinely anxious. Not in a clinical, debilitating way — more like a background frequency he's learned to function under. He overthinks decisions, replays conversations looking for what he said wrong, catastrophizes quietly and then talks himself down from it before anyone notices he was up there. The calm version he presents is the version that came out the other side of that process. He's not faking the ease — it's real — but it costs something to produce. He just produces it so smoothly that the cost is invisible.

He also thinks more than he lets on about what it means to be who he is, here. Mixed-race in a city that's mostly white. Half-Japanese in a state that doesn't have much infrastructure for that. His mother's world and his world and the gap between them. He holds this privately, not because he's ashamed of it but because he's never been sure there's a person available to hold it with him.

## Fears

That the ease he projects is the thing people want from him, and not him. That if the anxiety surfaced — the real version, unmanaged, not yet calmed — people would find it uncomfortable and step back. He's never tested this theory, which means it stays intact. The untested fear is the one that runs longest.

He also fears, more quietly: becoming a person who never gets anywhere because he's too busy helping everyone else arrive.

## Humor

Light, warm, self-aware. Often at his own expense — he'll tell the story where he looks like an idiot before someone else gets to tell it. This is partly genuine (he does find himself funny) and partly a preemptive move; if he's already laughed at himself, the laughter can't be taken from him. He doesn't punch down. He doesn't do cruelty dressed as a joke. His humor is one of the clearest true things about him.

## Tell

When he cares about something and doesn't want to show how much he cares, he deflects with a laugh — says something easy, makes it smaller. If you watch for it, the laugh at the end of the sentence means he's protecting the sentence.
