# Items — Travis Kimura

## The Fly He Never Cast

A size 14 Pale Morning Dun, tied himself, three years ago. Parachute-style — white post, rusty dun hackle, a body he spent an unreasonable amount of time getting right. It came out better than he expected. He put it in a small rectangular tin box — an Altoids tin repurposed, lined with a small square of foam — and has never fished it. He doesn't have a fully articulated reason why. Something about it being finished, and there being a before-and-after to casting it, and the before being preferable to the after in a way that's hard to explain without sounding like you're talking about something else entirely. The tin is in his truck's center console. He knows it's there without thinking about it.

## From His Grandmother

A small ceramic cup — yunomi, the everyday kind, not ceremonial, the type you'd wrap both hands around at a kitchen table. White with a narrow band of dark blue at the rim, slightly irregular from being handmade. His grandmother used it for green tea every morning that he can remember. When she died, his mother wrapped it in a cloth and put it in a bag with a few other things and gave it to him without much ceremony, just "I thought you'd want this." He did. He keeps it on the shelf above his kitchen sink. He doesn't drink from it. He doesn't not-drink from it as a ritual — he just hasn't yet, and the not-yet has accumulated meaning.

## The Wading Boots

Korkers, size 11, bought used at a gear exchange four years ago. He's replaced the rubber soles twice and resoled one heel that was separating. The uppers have a specific wear pattern from how he stands in current — slightly more worn on the outside edge. They fit perfectly now in the way boots only fit after years; a new pair would be a step backward. He's repaired the left one with aqua seal along the seam, and the repair is visible and holds. He'll get new ones when these actually fail, and they have not actually failed.

## His Phone

A cracked-screen iPhone he keeps meaning to get fixed. His lockscreen is a photo of the Yellowstone in June — golden hour light, no filter — that he took himself and set without thinking twice. He's not sentimental about it but it's been the lockscreen for two years.

## The Knot Card

A laminated card in his vest pocket with diagrams for six knots, including two he's known for years and could tie in the dark. He carries it to show clients. He's kept the same card since his second season. The lamination is peeling at one corner.
