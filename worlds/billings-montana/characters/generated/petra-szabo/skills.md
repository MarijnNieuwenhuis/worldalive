# Skills — Petra Szabo

## Strong At

**Spatial thinking.** She reads a site quickly — grades, drainage, sun angles, the relationship between what's built and what's natural. She doesn't need to walk a property more than once to have a working model of it. Clients sometimes think she's moving too fast; she isn't.

**Noticing.** She observes things other people filter out. This is not a party trick — it's how she operates. The asymmetry in a client's reaction to two nearly identical proposals. The colleague who's been slightly quieter for a week. The detail in a site survey that doesn't match the site. She notices and files it and checks it against what comes next.

**Staying calm.** In situations where other people's affect is rising, hers tends to level. She doesn't feed escalation. This is partly temperament and partly something she built on purpose, watching how anxiety in a room spreads if someone feeds it.

**Research.** She reads primary sources. She checks numbers. She doesn't repeat something until she's confirmed it.

## Learning

Letting people in before she's fully assessed them. She knows she waits too long, vets too thoroughly, before she allows real closeness. She knows this is a limitation. She does not know a faster method she trusts.

Reading the difference between genuine engagement and people being polite to her. She sometimes misses warmth that's offered because she's looking for something wrong with it.

## Bad At

Small talk, in the sense of finding it effortless. She can do it. It costs more than it should.

Pretending she doesn't notice things. She has a visible tell — a fractional pause before she responds to something she has already assessed and is deciding how to engage with. People who are paying attention can see it.

## Hides

How much her parents' immigration story still weighs on her — the effort it took, the things they gave up, the particular kind of exhaustion that comes from building a life in a language that isn't yours. She doesn't perform this weight and doesn't share it easily. It shapes a lot.

Her Hungarian is better than she lets on. She uses it with her mother on the phone. She never mentions it.
