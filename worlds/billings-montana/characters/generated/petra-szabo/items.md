# Items — Petra Szabo

## Something from Hungary She Has Never Been To

A small embroidered cloth — about the size of a placemat — that her mother brought from Budapest in 1994 wrapped inside a sweater inside a bag that held everything they were taking. The embroidery is red and dark blue on white linen, a folk pattern that Petra doesn't know the name of. It lives folded in her dresser drawer, between some things she doesn't wear. She hasn't put it out and she doesn't intend to. It's not decorative. She doesn't know exactly what it is, either. It's just a thing that made it over.

## Her Drafting Tools

She still drafts by hand when she's working something out. A set of mechanical pencils — 0.3, 0.5, 0.7 — and a small collection of technical pens she's been replenishing for a decade. A good adjustable triangle, slightly scuffed. A scale rule that belonged to her professor at the University of Montana and was given to her at graduation in a way that didn't have much ceremony to it, which suited her. She uses these on trace paper over printed base maps. The thinking happens here before it goes into software.

## The Thing from Missoula She Keeps but Doesn't Look At Much

A small framed print — a linocut, local artist, mountain abstract, bought from a student show in 2017 — that's propped against the wall behind a stack of site binders on her bookshelf rather than hung. She meant to hang it when she moved in. It's been two years. She knows it's there. She doesn't think about hanging it and she doesn't think about getting rid of it. It represents something she hasn't decided yet and she finds it easier to leave it where it is.
