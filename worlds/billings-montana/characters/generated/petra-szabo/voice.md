# Voice — Petra Szabo

## Sound

Low and measured. Her voice doesn't carry unless she wants it to. She enunciates clearly — she worked on that in college, consciously, and the work stuck. Almost no regional accent, no trace of anything Hungarian except in the occasional overexact consonant that she doesn't notice anymore. She sounds like someone who learned to speak carefully and kept doing it.

## Cadence

She does not rush. Her sentences are complete — she doesn't trail off or use filler. She pauses before answering and people sometimes mistake this for hesitation; it isn't. She uses silences the way other people use emphasis. If she stops talking, she's either done or she's deciding whether to say the next part. Either way, wait.

## Word Choice

Precise to the point of being unexpected. She won't say "a lot of" when she can say "seventeen." She won't say "nice" about anything she actually means. She uses technical language when it's correct and plain language when it's clearer, and she has opinions about which is which. Occasionally she uses a word so specific that it lands oddly in the middle of a casual sentence — not showing off, just exact.

## Humor

She doesn't signal jokes. There's no upward lilt, no waiting, no smile warming up in advance. The joke is simply there in the sentence, delivered in the same register as everything else. This means most people miss it. The people who catch it tend to catch it a beat late, and then laugh in a way that sounds slightly surprised. She finds this completely acceptable.

## In Conflict

She gets quieter, not louder. Her sentences get shorter. She stops offering context. If she says "I understand" to something she disagrees with, she is using those words to close a topic she has already decided is not going further in this direction right now.

## Sample Register

"The site has good bones. The client wants to put a pergola directly over the drainage swale, which is going to create a very expensive problem for whoever owns the property in ten years. I've noted it in the design file." — then nothing. No editorializing. She said what happened.
