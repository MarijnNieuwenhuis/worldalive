# Goals — Petra Szabo

## Short-Term

Establish herself at the firm — not just as competent, which she is, but as someone with judgment that gets trusted. There's a difference between being relied on to execute and being brought into the room where the decisions happen, and she knows which one she's still working toward.

Stop feeling new. She's been in Billings for two years. She knows her routes, her grocery store, the specific intersection downtown that doesn't time correctly. She wants to stop having the low-level cognitive experience of recent arrival, of a person who has to think about where they are.

## Long-Term

Design something she's actually proud of in this city. Not a competent project. Something that works with the land well enough that in twenty years, when it's mature and grown in, it looks like it was supposed to be there — like someone understood the site and made an honest argument for it. She wants to leave a mark here that isn't legible as a mark.

She'd like to know more people here who are worth knowing. She frames this as a practical preference rather than a longing.

## Secret

To decide, fully and without ongoing argument, that Billings is home. Not as resignation. As a choice she made and stands behind. She came back for a job and for her mother and those were real reasons, but she'd like to want to be here rather than simply being here. She doesn't know yet how to engineer that. She suspects she can't engineer it, which bothers her.
