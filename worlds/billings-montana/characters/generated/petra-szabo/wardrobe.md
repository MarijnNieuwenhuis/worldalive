# Wardrobe — Petra Szabo

## Overall Style

European-influenced minimalism without being precious about it. Clean lines, muted tones, quality over quantity. She buys fewer things and keeps them longer. She has opinions about fit and doesn't compromise on them, but she's not performing a look — she just doesn't want to think about getting dressed in the morning and have it be a problem. Her wardrobe is already solved.

## Work

Dark trousers — usually straight cut, usually charcoal, navy, or black. A good-quality button-down in a neutral: white, pale grey, chambray. Occasionally a fitted crewneck sweater in winter, wool or a wool blend. Everything coordinates without being matchy. Closed-toe shoes, always; she walks sites and doesn't want to explain her footwear choices. She has a pair of low-heeled boots that have made it through three winters and she intends to resoled them rather than replace them.

She wears a jacket to client presentations — structured, usually dark. It signals something she finds worth signaling without her having to say it.

## Casual

Well-fitting jeans, dark wash. A slightly oversized linen shirt in summer, a good sweater in winter. Leather sneakers in good condition. She doesn't dress down in a way that looks accidental — casual for her still has a deliberateness that people in Billings sometimes read as dressing up.

## Going Out

She doesn't go out frequently. When she does: a nicer version of what she already wears. A silk blouse she's had for five years and hasn't replaced. Dark trousers with a better drape. The same boots. She doesn't overhaul for a night; she just uses the better version of herself.

## Won't Wear

Logos. Anything with text on it. Novelty prints. Sneakers that look athletic when she's not being athletic. Colors that don't work with her coloring, which she assessed once a long time ago and doesn't revisit.

## Hands

The ring on her right hand — gold, small, Hungarian, her grandmother's. She doesn't talk about it. She sometimes turns it when she's thinking.

## Vulnerable / At Home

At home she wears the things that have gotten soft and don't look like much: an old university t-shirt from Missoula that she will not throw away, loose dark sweatpants, wool socks. This version of her wardrobe is extremely private. She would not answer the door in it.
