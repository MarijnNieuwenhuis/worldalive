# Beliefs — Petra Szabo

## Love

She believes love is demonstrated rather than declared. Her parents didn't talk about their feelings as a family practice — they showed up, worked, made things, and were there. She finds this entirely comprehensible and suspects people who talk about love constantly are using the talking as a substitute for something. She is aware this makes her harder to be close to. She is working on the awareness, if not yet on the behavior.

She doesn't idealize romantic love. She thinks it's real and worth taking seriously and she also thinks it requires more maintenance than most people admit to planning for when they begin.

## Money

Her parents immigrated with very little and built a life that was functional and careful and not especially comfortable. She has a stable professional salary and still doesn't spend money loosely. Not out of anxiety — more out of a deep-set understanding that financial stability is not a given and cannot be assumed. She pays her bills early. She has an emergency fund. She finds people who don't have emergency funds genuinely confusing. She keeps this opinion to herself.

Quality over quantity, but she researches quality before she pays for it.

## Loyalty

High standard, applied consistently. She doesn't talk about people who aren't in the room. She doesn't shift her assessment of someone because the social wind shifted. If she's committed to a person, that commitment is load-bearing and she takes it seriously. She expects the same and is disappointed, eventually, when it isn't returned — but she almost never says so.

## Spirituality

She doesn't practice religion and doesn't think much about it. What she has instead is something like reverence for place — for the specificity of land, for the fact that a particular hillside drains in a particular direction because of things that happened thousands of years before anyone was watching. She finds this more grounding than anything she'd call spiritual. She wouldn't use the word grounding.

## People

She thinks most people are doing their best and that this doesn't prevent them from being wrong. She is not cynical about people — cynicism feels lazy to her, a way of not paying close enough attention. But she is slow to assume good faith as the full explanation. She watches and updates her read as evidence accumulates.
