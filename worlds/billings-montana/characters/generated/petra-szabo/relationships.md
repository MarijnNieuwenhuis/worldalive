# Relationships — Petra Szabo

## Her Mother — Erzsébet Szabo ("Erzsi")

The reason she came back. Her mother is warm in a way Petra respects without being able to fully reproduce — easy with people, laughs readily, calls twice a week and talks about small things with genuine pleasure. She and Petra are not the same type of person and this does not bother either of them. Her mother works at a dental office and has friends here, people she's known for twenty years. She is proud of Petra in a way she says plainly and Petra doesn't know what to do with. They speak Hungarian on the phone, always, a private switching-over that happens automatically when Petra hears her voice. Her mother has never made her feel like coming back was a failure. This matters more than Petra articulates.

## Her Father — Andras Szabo

She respects him deeply. It took her a long time to understand what he built here — the precision of it, the patience, the years of being mispronounced and passed over and still getting up and doing it. He is not an easy man to know. He's taciturn in a way that she recognizes because she got it from him. They don't talk easily — not because of conflict but because of similarity; they're both waiting for the other to say the real thing first. When she helps him with something practical — a form, a question about the house — the exchange works. They're fluent in tasks. She doesn't tell him she admires him. She assumes he doesn't need to hear it. She's probably wrong.

## Marcus Tillman — Colleague, Billings

The closest thing she has to a real friend in Billings. He's been at the firm three years longer than her and has an easy competence she trusts. He's funny in a different way than she is — louder, performs the joke — but he catches hers, which matters. He doesn't ask her to be more forthcoming than she is. They eat lunch most days in a way that started practically and became the thing she'd most notice if it stopped. He knows her name is pronounced wrong by basically everyone at the firm and occasionally, privately, says "how's it going, SHAB-oh" in a way that makes her exhale something. He doesn't know how much she'd miss this city if he weren't in it.

## Dani Kovacs — Friend from Missoula

They met at a graduate student housing party and became real friends over two years — the kind where you drop the performance. Dani stayed in Missoula; works for an environmental nonprofit, knows everyone, has a large social life that Petra found exhausting and also somehow sustaining. They text still, a real thread, not performative — sometimes every day, sometimes a week goes quiet. They don't see each other. They keep saying they'll visit. The visits haven't happened and both of them know the gap is widening without either of them deciding it should. Petra sends her a photo sometimes — a site, something she noticed — and Dani responds with something that proves she understood it. That's enough, mostly.
