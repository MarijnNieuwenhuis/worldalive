# Cassidy Lane — Goals

## Short-term
Save enough to move out of her mom's place — not because she resents her mom, but because she needs a door she can close. They've been roommates since she graduated high school and it's fine and it's also slowly grinding her down in ways she can't fully name. She'd also like to take a real trip somewhere outside Montana, even just once — not necessarily far, just somewhere she chose on purpose, not somewhere she ended up.

## Long-term
Unclear. She hasn't let herself picture it. Sometimes late at night after a closing shift she gets close to it — something with her own place, maybe something that isn't behind a bar, maybe a version of herself that's built something rather than just maintained — but then the shift starts and the bar fills up and she gets good at her job again and the picture fades. She doesn't think she's incapable of wanting more. She thinks she's been too busy surviving to get around to it.

## Secret
She wants someone to choose her. Specifically, on purpose, with full knowledge of what they're getting. Not because she's convenient or available or fun in the short term — because they looked at her and decided yes, that one, and meant it. She's been the person people pick up and put down. She's been good for right now. She'd like, once, to be someone's long-term answer, and she wants it with a kind of quiet ache she would never say out loud because it feels like too much to admit, too soft, too earnest for the version of herself she shows the world.
