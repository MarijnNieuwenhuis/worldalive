# Cassidy Lane — Items

She carries almost nothing. Her tips go in her back right pocket, folded once — she counts them in the car before she drives home. A Chapstick in her front pocket, worn flat on one side from being pressed against her thigh. A scratched-up iPhone she keeps meaning to replace the screen on.

The lighter she doesn't smoke. It's a Zippo, brushed silver, with a small dent in one corner where it got dropped on a tile floor. It belonged to her dad — not the good version of him, just him, the version she actually had. She took it off the kitchen counter the day he moved out and she's carried it since she was eighteen. She doesn't think of it as sentimental. She thinks of it as a reminder to not make his choices, which is almost the same thing but feels different to hold.

At home, on her nightstand: a photograph from a disposable camera, printed at a CVS and never framed. It's from the summer she was nineteen — her and her best friend Dani at Pictograph Cave, both squinting into the sun, both laughing at something just off frame. Cassidy is pointing at something. Neither of them remembers what. It's a good picture of both of them, the kind you only get when nobody was trying for it.
