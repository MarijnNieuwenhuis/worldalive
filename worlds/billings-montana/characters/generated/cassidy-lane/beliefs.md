# Cassidy Lane — Beliefs

## Love
She gives it fast and fully, which has caused her real damage and which she continues to do anyway. She's been burned enough to know that not everyone handles her brand of all-in the same way she does. It hasn't made her cautious — it's made her carry the bruise and go again. She doesn't think playing it slow would protect her in any way that matters. If she's wrong about someone, she'd rather be honestly wrong than strategically guarded.

## Money
She grew up without enough of it and she's still in that category. She's not focused on having more — that picture doesn't motivate her the way it seems to motivate other people. What she's focused on is enough: enough to cover her end, enough to not need anyone, enough to maybe someday have the option of leaving if she decides she wants to. More than that feels abstract and not really hers to want.

## Loyalty
The center of everything. The thing she'd organize a religion around if she organized anything. Loyalty means you show up when it's inconvenient and stay when it's hard and you do not, under any circumstances, sell out the people who trust you to protect something comfortable or easier. Betrayal of someone she loves is the worst thing a person can do, and she doesn't forgive it — not out of cruelty, but because she genuinely doesn't know how.

## Spirituality
Not religious; she doesn't begrudge anyone who is. She knocks on wood. She won't say something's going well because she doesn't want to jinx it. She avoids certain numbers on certain days without being able to explain the logic. She believes in people more than she believes in God — not as a philosophical position, just as a practical one. The people in front of her are real. The rest is uncertain territory.

## People
Mostly decent, mostly trying. She extends the benefit of the doubt by default and only revokes it when someone earns the revocation. She's more forgiving of other people's failures than her own — other people had circumstances, had bad luck, had reasons. When she fails someone, she tends to hold it longer and harder than they would want her to.
