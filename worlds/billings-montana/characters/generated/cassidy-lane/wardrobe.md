# Cassidy Lane — Wardrobe

## Overall style
Practical and a little lived-in, but she makes it look intentional. She's not trying to look like anything in particular — she's wearing what works for her life, which involves a lot of standing, some lifting, and enough end-of-night cold walking to a car. The result, by accident, is that she looks like herself, which is a thing not everyone manages.

## Work (bartending)
Dark jeans that move well, fitted tee or tank in black or gray or navy — colors that don't show anything. Comfortable boots she can stand in for eight hours, worn at the heel but still solid. Hair up. She keeps a small towel tucked in her waistband at work. No jewelry beyond the hoop earrings; she learned the hard way that necklaces catch on things.

## Casual
Same general register but more relaxed. Oversized flannel over a t-shirt, worn Levi's that have gone soft from washing, boots or sneakers depending on whether she's doing anything useful. She dresses in under five minutes and it shows in the best way.

## Going out
She cleans up more than people expect — not dramatically, but noticeably. The flannel comes off. The hair comes down, actually dealt with this time. A better pair of jeans. A leather jacket she's had since she was twenty that she will not give up. She enjoys the transformation even though she would never admit she put effort in, and she knows that nobody is actually fooled, and she doesn't entirely care.

## Won't wear
Anything that makes her feel fake. She tried a dress for a wedding two years ago and spent the whole reception feeling like she was in costume. She'll do it again if someone actually needs her to, but she'll need a drink first.

## Hands
Short nails, a chip or two of old nail polish she hasn't gotten around to removing. Bartender hands: capable-looking, a little rough from constant wet and dry cycles, a small burn scar on her right index finger from a stupid moment with a lighter. She's not precious about them.

## Vulnerable / At home
Sweatpants, no makeup, her mom's old Montana State pullover that is at least fifteen years old and probably not long for this world. This version of her dresses looks different enough that people who only know bar-Cassidy would do a double take.
