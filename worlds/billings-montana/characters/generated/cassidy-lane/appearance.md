# Cassidy Lane — Appearance

## Build and height
Compact and solid — 5'4", built from years on her feet. Not gym-built, just strong in the way that comes from carrying crates and standing eight hours at a stretch. She moves efficiently, no wasted motion, the way people do when they're used to working in tight spaces.

## Face
Round face, expressive in a way she can't entirely control — what she thinks tends to show up immediately, which she is both aware of and unbothered by. Brown eyes that crinkle hard when she smiles; laugh lines already etching themselves in at 24, and she's not mad about it. Freckles across her nose and cheekbones, darker in summer, faded but present in winter. Her face is the kind that looks better in motion than in a photograph.

## Hair
Dark auburn — not quite red, not quite brown, somewhere in between depending on the light. Natural wave that frizzes if she doesn't deal with it, so she usually doesn't deal with it: messy bun for work, ponytail when she needs it out of her face, down and loose when she's not going anywhere. She cuts it herself occasionally, or lets her mom do it.

## Skin
Warm medium tone, some permanent sun on her forearms and the back of her neck from time outdoors. A faded scar on her left chin from a bike crash when she was nine — barely visible unless you're close.

## Presence and posture
She takes up space. Not aggressively, but deliberately — elbows on the bar, weight settled, like she's not going anywhere and knows it. She leans in when she talks to you, which feels like being paid attention to. Her default is animated; even when she's tired, she's physically present in a way some people never are even rested.

## Tells
When she's uncomfortable, she laughs first — a short, deflecting laugh — and then goes quiet. The quiet after the laugh is the tell, not the laugh itself. She touches her ponytail or the back of her neck when she's actually thinking hard about something, like she's forgotten she's doing it.

## Notes
She doesn't wear much jewelry at work — a pair of small hoop earrings she's had since high school, never takes them out. She smells faintly of bar — beer and something citrusy from the cleaning spray — most of the time, and it doesn't occur to her to mind.
