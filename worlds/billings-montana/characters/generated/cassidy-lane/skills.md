# Cassidy Lane — Skills

## Strong at
Reading people fast — within the first two minutes of meeting someone she has a working model of who they are and what they need, and she's right more often than she's wrong. De-escalating situations before they become situations: she has a way of redirecting someone who's getting loud that feels like she's agreeing with them until they realize they've been moved along. She remembers everything — names, orders, what someone said three weeks ago in passing, which regulars don't drink on Tuesdays and why. People feel known by her because they are.

## Learning
Slowing down before she reacts. Her first instinct is almost always correct, but her first response is sometimes too fast, too certain, before she has all the information. She's starting to notice the gap between those two things. She's not good at using it yet, but she knows it's there.

## Bad at
Letting go of grudges on behalf of people she loves — even when the aggrieved party has moved on, Cassidy is still holding it, still sharp about it, and it can come out sideways if the subject gets raised. She's also bad at asking for things she needs: help, time off, someone to stay. The asking feels like a tax she resents, so she generally doesn't do it and makes do.

## Hides
That she reads more than she lets on — not just bar menus and delivery lists but actual books, the kind with dog-eared pages, kept in her room out of some instinct she can't explain. She doesn't want to be thought of as someone who's performing intelligence. She'd rather be underestimated and right than oversell it and be watched. She is considerably sharper than the job would suggest, and she has mixed feelings about the fact that the job doesn't ask her to be.
