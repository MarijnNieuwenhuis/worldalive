# Jolene Voss — History

*Chronological timeline. Updated when stories are ingested.*

## [~Age 12] The Necklace

Jolene broke her mother's favorite necklace while playing with it without permission. Instead of admitting it, she hid the pieces at the back of a drawer. Her mother searched for it for months, blamed herself for losing it, and never found it. Jolene said nothing. She found the pieces again years later.

The guilt of getting away with it was worse than getting caught would have been. That quiet, sustained shame became the foundation of her honesty — not a natural trait, but a choice she made after living with what it felt like to lie and be believed.

There's a quiet symmetry she's probably never consciously noticed: her defining shame is about a hidden broken necklace. Her most precious possession is a necklace she wears every day.

## [Early 20s] Arleen's Death

Arleen — Jolene's closest friend, the one person she was fully herself around — died in a car accident. Jolene fell apart, then slowly began to piece herself back together. She's still in that process.

The loss is not fully resolved. Something about it remains unspoken or unfaced — a thread that will surface in stories. She wears the necklace Arleen gave her every day.
