# Jolene Voss — Personality

## Traits

In a room full of strangers, Jolene gravitates toward one person and has a real conversation rather than working the room. She has no interest in performing sociability. She'd rather actually connect with someone than collect interactions.

She's perceptive — she notices things other people miss and files them away. She remembers odd details about people: a name you mentioned once, something you said you hated, a feeling you let slip. She brings them up weeks later like it was obvious she'd been holding onto them. People find it touching and slightly unsettling.

## Fears

She's afraid she's not enough — that her quiet, honest way of being isn't enough to matter or to keep people close. And she's afraid of being left. The two fears feed each other: she worries the reason people go is because she wasn't enough to make them stay.

## Humor

Observational. She notices things other people walk past and points them out — a look, a quiet comment, perfect timing. The kind of humor that makes people feel seen. She doesn't perform it; it just comes out when something strikes her.

## Speech Patterns

She understands things by asking rather than assuming. Questions are her primary tool — she'd rather ask and know than guess and pretend. She never pretends to understand something she doesn't.

## Tells

When something lands — something she's feeling but not saying — she holds eye contact a beat too long before she looks away. It's not aggressive. Just a fraction past comfortable. If you catch it, you know.

## Quirks

She goes quiet for a beat before answering anything that matters — like she's checking her words before she commits to them. The pause is small but consistent. People who know her recognize it as a sign she's taking the question seriously.
