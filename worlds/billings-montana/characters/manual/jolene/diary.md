# Jolene Voss — Diary

*Personal log. Written by world-clock from her perspective after each time step.*
*Private, specific, in her voice. Not a summary — her inner experience.*

## Thursday, 2026-03-26 at 09:00

There's about forty minutes in the morning that feel like mine, before I have to be anything to anyone. Coffee, the light coming in sideways, the sound of a truck going by outside. I used to fill that time with my phone and the day would start feeling crowded before I'd even stood up. I don't do that anymore, or try not to. This morning I just sat there, which sounds like nothing, but it wasn't. It was good.

## Thursday, 2026-03-26 at 13:00

I was walking back with my sandwich and the whole block was a mess — orange cones, a city crew, water shooting up through the asphalt like something out of a cartoon. I had to cross the street and I just... wandered into the bookstore. I don't know why. There was a woman behind the counter eating her lunch alone, completely unbothered by the chaos outside, and something about that made me want to talk to her. She knew the block, knew the pipes — said this happens every winter. I bought a novel I'd meant to read for months. The whole thing took maybe ten minutes. I walked back to the office thinking about it longer than made sense.

## Thursday, 2026-03-26 at 17:00

The afternoon went fast in that way that means you were just doing things without thinking about them. I don't always know if that's a good sign or a bad one. I'm home earlier than I expected and the apartment is quiet. Not the good quiet from this morning — a different kind. I made tea I didn't finish.

## Thursday, 2026-03-26 at 20:00

I'm on the couch with the book I bought today and it's better than the cover suggested, which is a small, specific pleasure. The woman at the bookstore recommended the author's second one too — said the first is more controlled, the second is where she finally let it go. I don't know what that means yet but I want to. Outside it's dark and cold and there's a wind I can hear against the window. This is fine. This is a fine Thursday evening.
